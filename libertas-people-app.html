Libertas People staff app

Designing a survey app

slavefreetrade is a rightstech NGO on a mission to deliver human rights to workplaces globally. They do it with the help of their core product Libertas - a human source intelligence system that discovers and maps supply chains, and assesses and monitors workplace conditions in real time.

An important part of Libertas is the survey that collects the information from employees across workplaces. So far this survey has been sent via e-mail and other distribution methods but the next step was to create an app to make the survey process more convenient.

Goal

Design the user experience and interface for the survey app that is used by employees across workplaces. The main function of the app is to provide the survey to the users - it will collect information from employees via monthly surveys which will then be analyzed and tracked with the Libertas dashboard.

Scope and timetable of the project

To design the first iteration and MVP of the app in 3 weeks. After the MVP, the app will be further developed to include even more functionalities for employees and companies. The design work involved creating the user flow, information architecture and the high-fidelity wireframes for the app.

Team

Team: Yina Smith-Danenhower (UX/UI), Katri Eliander (UX/UI), Peter Irving (Flutter developer) & dev team, a Product Manager and Marketing.

Yina and I were responsible for the entire design process. We defined the user flow and IA together and the wireframe designs we divided between us.

Tools

Figma, Zeplin, pen & paper

Understand & Define

The target audience and business requirements

We started by getting an understanding of the target audience. The app's target user is anyone who is working in the supply chains of the companies or organizations that are using the Libertas human rights system. The target audience is hence a broad group of users with different levels of tech-savviness. It is therefore critical that the app is very simple, easy to use and accessible. We knew the following about the app and its requirements when we started:

  • Native app for Android / iOS (will be developed with Flutter).
  • The core function of the app is to provide the survey to the user.
  • The app will be downloaded by employees in workplaces via an invitation link.
  • The app and survey are completely anonymous so no information given can be traced back to the user.
  • The survey is monthly - every month there will be a new set of questions for the user to fill in.
  • The app design needs to be simple and it has to be easy to use.

User flow

As the survey appears monthly on the app for one year's time, users will return to the app each month to fill in the latest survey. The first survey contains 20 binary questions and the next 11 surveys contain 10 questions each.

For new users the user flow will start when they open the app and for returning users the user flow starts with the push notification that informs them that a new survey is available to be filled in. The success criteria for both users is to successfully fill in the survey.

Entry point: Open the app (first-time user) or push notification (returning user)

Success criteria: Finishes survey

Ideation & Iteration

Information architecture

The next step was to determine the IA of the app. The app needed to remain as simple as possible as the main task for the user is to complete the survey. In addition to the survey, we decided to add an informative "About" page so that users are able to find a bit more information within the app. We didn't want them to have to move away from the app to read more about slavefreetrade as an organization and the survey.

Together with the Product manager and the marketing team we decided on an information architecture shown below. We went a couple of times back and forth about having a "Profile" section but in the end it was decided (with input from the Director in charge of the research and surveys) that a profile is not necessary in the MVP.

The app has hence four main sections: Onboarding, Home screen, Survey screens and About screen.

From sketches to high-fidelity iterations

I was responsible for designing the home screen and the survey screens. Below are the first sketches that I did for the user flow.

The home screen needed to be easy to understand with one primary call-to-action "Take survey". I iterated many times the home screen due to for instance the changes we made in the information architecture.

  • Accessibility: I changed the icons so that they would be more describing and make the text content more easily understandable.
  • Navigation: When we still planned to include a "Profile" section, we added a tab bar in the app to make it easy for users to navigate between Home, About and Profile screens. However, as we in the end decided to skip the profile section in the MVP, we removed the tab bar to simplify the navigation. A tab bar would have been unnecessary with so few screens. Now, users can navigate to the About screens from the Home screen.

Below are iterations of the home screen for first-time users that are about to fill in their first survey. For returning users, the screen will look slightly different. The text on the top will be replaced with a notification box that tells the user that a new survey is available.
